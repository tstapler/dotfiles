# Mission, Vision & Tenets — Reference

## Collins Framework

**Built to Last / Good to Great** — Vision = Core Values + Core Purpose + BHAG

Key principles:
- Values are **discovered, not designed**. "You cannot set organizational values, you can only discover them."
- "Core values and purpose, if properly conceived, remain fixed. Everything else should be open for change."
- The Mars Group exercise: send 5-7 people who best embody the org to another planet. Their behaviors = your values.
- Offsite time allocation: 70-80% on alignment gaps, 10-20% on documentation. Most orgs invert this.

**BHAG (Big Hairy Audacious Goal):**
- "Clear and compelling, needing little explanation; people get it right away"
- Finite — the org will know when achieved
- "We choose to go to the moon" is the archetype: specific, audacious, time-bound, no ambiguity about success

## Lencioni Framework

**HBR "Make Your Values Mean Something" (2002)**

Four types most orgs conflate:
1. **Core** — genuinely held, non-negotiable, typically 2-3, already being lived
2. **Aspirational** — desired but absent; belongs in hiring strategy or OKR, not on a plaque
3. **Permission-to-Play** — ethical baseline every decent company has
4. **Accidental** — emerged from founding team personality

"To stop everything while you spend days drafting a 'values statement' is not the most effective use of time. The document is a very small fraction of the work."

## Amazon Leadership Principles

Format: Label (1 word) + 2-4 behavior sentences in third person ("Leaders are owners. They think long term...")

Structural patterns:
- Every sentence starts with "they" or "leaders" — purely behavioral
- Frequent negative framing: "They never say 'that's not my job'"
- Visible tradeoffs: "Think Big" vs. "Frugality" vs. "Deliver Results" are genuinely in tension
- Operationalized via hiring bar raisers, performance reviews, promotion criteria — infrastructure matters as much as content

Count: 16 (widely regarded as too many; original 14 are more credible than the 2 recently added)

Notable LPs (most cited, each 2-3 sentences):
- **Customer Obsession**: starts from customer, works backwards; earns trust vs. just attention
- **Ownership**: act beyond your own team; "that's not my job" doesn't exist
- **Bias for Action**: speed matters; most decisions reversible; default to action
- **Frugality**: accomplish more with less; constraints breed resourcefulness

## Netflix Culture

Structure: 4 meta-principles + 8 embedded values

What makes it credible:
- Includes uncomfortable implications: "We model ourselves on a professional sports team, not a family"
- Values with exit criteria: "If you'd find it hard to support the breadth of our slate, Netflix is probably not the best place for you"
- Describes what values look like in violation, not just in practice
- Long-form but coherent — every claim traces to the founding mission

Core meta-principles:
1. Dream Team (high performance, high pay, fast removal of poor fit)
2. Context not Control (give people context to make good decisions rather than rules)
3. Highly Aligned, Loosely Coupled (aligned on goals, autonomous on methods)
4. Seek to Entertain (the mission colors every decision about content breadth)

## Stripe Operating Principles

Format: Label sentence + 2-3 behavior sentences

Count: 6 (optimal density for recall)

What makes it work:
- Second layer of shorthand phrases ("We haven't won yet", "Disagree and commit", "Really, really, really care") — the shorthand is what gets invoked in meetings
- Sequenced logically: Users → Craft → Speed → Collaboration → Talent → Curiosity
- Uses contrast: "Whereas many companies get slower until mired in mediocrity, we aspire to become the world's fastest company"
- "We are exothermic and macro-optimistic" — distinctive enough to be unmistakably Stripe

## Tenet Format Examples

**Amazon style** (most behavioral):
> Ownership: Leaders are owners. They think long term and don't sacrifice long-term value for short-term results. They act on behalf of the entire company, beyond just their own team. They never say 'that's not my job.'

**Stripe style** (contrast-forward):
> Move with urgency and focus. We move with speed on what matters most and take the time to invest in what will make us faster tomorrow. Whereas many companies get slower until they're eventually mired in mediocrity, we aspire to become the world's fastest company.

**Netflix style** (includes violation):
> Judgment: You make wise decisions despite ambiguity. You identify root causes, and get beyond treating symptoms. You think strategically, and can articulate what you are, and are not, trying to do. You are good at using data to inform your intuition.

## Tenet Count Research

Cognitive basis for 1-3 sentences:
- Miller (1956): working memory holds 4±2 chunks — constrains both number of tenets (≤7) and complexity per tenet
- The label is what gets invoked in real-time; the paragraph is what you read once to understand
- If the full text must be read to apply the value, it hasn't been internalized
- Amazon's "They never say 'that's not my job'" (7 words) is more clarifying than a paragraph of qualifiers

Optimal count evidence:
- Stripe: 6 — highest practitioner credibility
- Netflix: 8 embedded — works because operationalized with examples and exit criteria
- Amazon: 16 — widely regarded as too many; only persists due to extraordinary institutional investment
- General: 5-8 sweet spot, 3-6 high-credibility zone
